Understanding the Wendler 5/3/1 Program

The Wendler 5/3/1 Program is a strength training regimen designed by Jim Wendler. It's a simple, effective, and flexible program aimed at increasing your one-rep max (1RM) in key lifts over time. The program focuses on four main compound lifts: Squat, Bench Press, Deadlift, and Overhead Press.

Program Structure
  • 4-Week Cycle: Each cycle consists of three training weeks followed by a deload week.
  • Progressive Overload: Gradually increasing the weights to build strength.
  • Rep Scheme: The numbers 5, 3, and 1 refer to the reps performed at different percentages of your 1RM.
Weekly Breakdown
Week Set 1 Set 2 Set 3 Set 4
Week 1 65% x 5 75% x 5 85% x 5+ ---
Week 2 70% x 3 80% x 3 90% x 3+ ---
Week 3 75% x 5 85% x 3 95% x 1+ ---
Week 4 (Deload) 40% x 5 50% x 5 60% x 5 ---

The "+" sign indicates that you should perform as many reps as possible (AMRAP) while maintaining good form. After completing each 4-week cycle, you increase your training max (usually 5-10 lbs) and start the next cycle, ensuring continuous progress.

Key Principles
  • Simplicity: Focuses on a few core lifts without excessive volume.
  • Consistency: Regularly following the cycle for long-term gains.
  • Adaptability: Can be tailored to individual needs and schedules.

By adhering to the Wendler 5/3/1 Program, you can systematically build strength, prevent plateaus, and achieve significant improvements in your main lifts.
